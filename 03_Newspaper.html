For nearly 500 years, kings, Parliamentarians, business executives, worldly men and women have turned to ''The Prince,'' by Niccolo Machiavelli, for advice on the ins and outs of getting power and keeping it. Now there's even a version for children. Claudia Hart, a visual artist who lives in New York City, has combined maxims from ''The Prince'' with illustrations from such children's classics as ''The Little Prince'' and ''Peter Rabbit'' in ''A Child's Machiavelli'' (Viking Penguin).

Just how relevant are the experiences of a 16th-century former emissary of the Florentine Republic to the schoolyards of today? Judge for yourself.
